Purchasing private residential property in Singapore

Taiwan national · US national · Dual passport holder

Strategic recommendation — dual passport holders

Purchase under your US passport. The USSFTA (2004) entitles US nationals to the same ABSD treatment as Singapore citizens — 0% on a first residential property, versus 60% as a Taiwan national. On a S$3M purchase this is a S$1.8M difference. Instruct your conveyancing lawyer to file the ABSD remission form when exercising the OTP, and present your US passport as the primary identity document throughout.

If you already own Singapore residential property purchased under either passport, consult your lawyer on how prior ownership affects your ABSD tier before proceeding.

Section 1

Eligibility

Both Under the Residential Property Act, both Taiwan and US nationals may purchase private condominiums and apartments without restriction. Neither may purchase landed property (terrace, semi-detached, detached) or HDB flats without LDAU approval, which is rarely granted to non-PRs.

The Meyer Road corridor projects in Document 1B are private condominiums and fall within this permitted category for both nationalities.

Section 4

Legal representation and process

A Singapore-qualified conveyancing lawyer is required for every purchase. They handle OTP review, title searches, stamp duty filing, and coordination with the Singapore Land Authority (SLA). OTP to completion is typically 10–12 weeks.

OTP option fee1% of purchase price, paid to seller on receiving the OTP
OTP exercise fee4–9% of purchase price, paid within the option period (typically 21 days)
Stamp duty deadlineBSD and ABSD must be paid within 14 days of exercising the OTP
Completion period10–12 weeks from OTP exercise to key handover
USAdditional step for US nationals: Instruct your lawyer to file Form ABSD(REM) within 14 days of exercising the OTP to claim the FTA remission. This filing is not automatic — it must be explicitly instructed. Provide a valid US passport and a statutory declaration of US citizenship at the time of filing.

US rental income reporting

Singapore rental income must be declared on Schedule E of your US federal return. Claim a Foreign Tax Credit for the 24% Singapore income tax paid — this may largely offset US federal tax on the same income.

USWorldwide income reporting: US citizens must declare Singapore rental income on their federal return annually. The 24% Singapore tax paid qualifies for the Foreign Tax Credit and will often fully offset federal tax on that income. Capital gains from eventual sale must also be reported on your US return even though Singapore levies no CGT — gains are taxed at US capital gains rates (0–20% + potential 3.8% NIIT). Factor this into your exit model.

No capital gains tax (Singapore)

Gains are fully repatriable after clearing outstanding loans, SSD, and taxes. Proceeds may be wired back to Taiwan or the US without restriction.

Trading intent risk

IRAS may assess frequent short-term buyers as traders and tax profits as income rather than capital gains. Both nationalities face this equally.

USUS capital gains on Singapore property: Even though Singapore levies no CGT, US citizens must report the gain on their federal return. Taxed at capital gains rates (0–20%) plus potential 3.8% NIIT. Singapore's 0% CGT cannot offset this — no Foreign Tax Credit is available against a tax Singapore did not charge. Build this into exit modelling, particularly on high-appreciation scenarios.
